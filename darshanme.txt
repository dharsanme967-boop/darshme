R is a free, open-source programming language and software environment specifically designed for statistical computing, data analysis, and data visualization. It is widely used by data scientists, statisticians, and researchers across academia and industry. 
Key Features
Open-Source and Free: R is available as free software under the GNU General Public License, with its source code accessible to everyone, fostering a large and active community of users and developers.
Comprehensive Statistical Tools: R offers an extensive, built-in collection of tools for various statistical analyses, including linear and nonlinear modeling, time-series analysis, classification, and clustering.
Powerful Graphics Capabilities: One of R's strengths is the ease with which it can produce high-quality, customizable, publication-ready plots and graphics, often using packages like ggplot2 and lattice.
Vast Package Ecosystem: The core functionality of R is extended by a massive repository of user-created packages, the Comprehensive R Archive Network (CRAN), which hosts over 20,000 packages for specialized tasks, from bioinformatics to financial analysis.
Cross-Platform Compatibility: R runs on a wide variety of operating systems, including Windows, macOS, and Linux.
